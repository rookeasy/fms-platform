# FMS-0026 Digital Project Closeout & Building Handover Standard

FMS-0026 defines the documentation-only standard for how completed Fuzion Fire prime contracts should close out digitally, transition into Building Protection Passport™ records, and begin long-term lifecycle relationships with Fuzion Fire Service and FMS.

No code, schema, workflow automation, QR generation, dashboard, CRM, billing, membership, inspection, report, certificate, or client portal implementation is included in this phase.

## Purpose

The purpose of FMS-0026 is to define a standard Digital Project Closeout Package and building handover process for Fuzion Fire prime contracts.

The standard ensures that project closeout is not only an administrative endpoint. It becomes the moment when the building is organized, handed over, made Passport-ready, and prepared for service, membership, and lifecycle follow-up.

## Vision

Every completed Fuzion Fire prime contract shall conclude with a standardized Digital Project Closeout Package that transitions the building into its Building Protection Passport™ and initiates its long-term lifecycle relationship with Fuzion Fire Service and FMS.

The closeout package should become a premium client experience and an internal operating standard. It should make Fuzion Fire easier to trust, easier to re-engage, and easier to choose for future service and advisory work.

## Strategic Objectives

- Standardize the digital handover experience for every completed prime contract.
- Preserve project knowledge as structured building intelligence.
- Create a permanent Building Protection Passport™ foundation.
- Improve client confidence at turnover.
- Support P.Eng., contractor, warranty, product, and as-built documentation review.
- Prepare service teams for ITM transition.
- Prepare sales teams for FMS membership and lifecycle conversion conversations.
- Reduce document loss, closeout confusion, and inconsistent handover quality.
- Create a repeatable source of future service, advisory, and capital planning opportunities.

## Closeout Philosophy

Digital closeout should be treated as the first step in the building's long-term fire protection lifecycle.

Fuzion Fire should not hand over a pile of disconnected files. It should hand over an organized building record that explains what was installed, where it is located, what records matter, what obligations remain, what warranties apply, what inspections are expected, and how the client can continue working with Fuzion Fire after project completion.

The closeout package should be:

- Structured
- Searchable
- Building-specific
- Client-friendly
- Internally useful
- Passport-ready
- Service-transition ready
- Lifecycle-conversion ready

## Required Deliverables

Each Digital Project Closeout Package should include, when applicable:

- Building profile summary
- Project summary
- Building Protection Passport™ contents
- P.Eng. compliance package
- Contractor compliance package
- As-built documentation
- Product information
- Asset register
- Warranty package
- Recommended inspection schedule
- Service transition summary
- FMS membership transition summary
- QR code or future access workflow
- Handover meeting record
- Internal lifecycle conversion notes

When a deliverable is not applicable or not available, the closeout package should record that status rather than leaving ambiguity.

## Building Protection Passport Contents

The Building Protection Passport™ should contain the curated building records that are appropriate for long-term owner, manager, and service use.

Recommended Passport contents:

- Building identity and address
- Owner, property manager, site, and AHJ contacts
- Fire protection system summary
- Asset register
- Client-visible drawing metadata
- Approved or as-built document metadata
- Compliance and test document metadata
- Warranty and product information metadata
- Service transition notes
- Recommended inspection and maintenance schedule
- Handover summary
- QR code or future access reference

Internal-only opportunity, pricing, sales, or risk notes must not be included in client-visible Passport content.

## P.Eng. Compliance Package

The P.Eng. compliance package should collect engineering records required to support professional review, compliance assurance, and future building reference.

Potential contents:

- Engineering letters
- Stamped drawings
- Design briefs
- Hydraulic calculations
- Code or standard references
- Engineering review notes
- Alternative solution references if applicable
- Professional sign-off records
- Engineering change summaries

The package should clearly distinguish final issued records from drafts, working notes, and superseded versions.

## Contractor Compliance Package

The contractor compliance package should collect records produced or coordinated by Fuzion Fire and relevant trade partners.

Potential contents:

- Contractor material and test certificates
- Installation declarations
- Commissioning records
- Test results
- Manufacturer startup records
- Backflow test records
- Fire pump acceptance records
- Alarm verification references
- Deficiency closeout confirmations where applicable
- Subcontractor closeout records

These records should support both owner confidence and future service readiness.

## As-Built Documentation

As-built documentation should describe the final installed fire protection condition.

Recommended records:

- As-built drawings
- Approved shop drawings
- Riser diagrams
- Floor plans
- Zone maps
- Fire department connection locations
- Control valve locations
- Fire pump room layouts
- Standpipe and sprinkler coverage notes
- Change summaries from issued-for-construction documents

As-built records should be versioned clearly and marked as final only when reviewed.

## Product Information

Product information should help owners, managers, and service teams understand the installed systems and components.

Recommended records:

- Manufacturer data sheets
- Product manuals
- Installation manuals
- Maintenance manuals
- Valve, pump, backflow, alarm, suppression, and specialty equipment documentation
- Spare parts information
- Recommended service intervals
- Manufacturer contact or support references

Product information should be linked to assets where practical in future implementation.

## Asset Register

The closeout package should include a building-specific asset register.

Recommended asset register fields:

- Asset name
- Asset type
- Location
- Manufacturer
- Model
- Serial number where available
- Installation date
- Warranty expiry date
- Inspection frequency
- Condition or commissioning status
- Related document references
- Notes for service transition

The asset register should be complete enough to support ITM scoping and future Passport workflows.

## Warranty Package

The warranty package should identify what is covered, who owns the obligation, and when coverage expires.

Recommended warranty contents:

- Warranty summary
- Equipment warranty records
- Installation warranty records
- Manufacturer warranty terms
- Contractor warranty terms
- Start and expiry dates
- Claim contact information
- Exclusions or limitations
- Required maintenance conditions
- Warranty-related product documentation

Warranty records should support both client service and internal follow-up.

## Inspection Schedule

The closeout package should provide a recommended inspection and maintenance schedule based on installed systems, applicable standards, and service transition needs.

Potential schedule elements:

- Annual sprinkler inspection
- Fire pump testing
- Backflow testing
- Standpipe testing
- Fire alarm coordination references
- Emergency lighting checks
- Fire extinguisher service
- Special suppression system service
- Seasonal or occupancy-specific requirements

FMS-0026 does not implement inspections or schedules. It defines the handover expectation that this information should be captured for future service workflows.

## Service Transition

Service transition should connect project closeout to Fuzion Fire Service.

The transition should include:

- Building service summary
- Installed system summary
- Recommended ITM scope
- Asset register review
- Known access considerations
- Key site contacts
- Warranty considerations
- First service follow-up recommendation
- Internal service handoff owner
- Client introduction path

The goal is to make it easy for Fuzion Fire Service to understand the building and propose or begin appropriate recurring service.

## FMS Membership Transition

FMS membership transition should identify whether the building is a strong candidate for membership and Passport access.

The transition should include:

- Passport readiness status
- Client-visible record readiness
- Membership fit notes
- Suggested membership value proposition
- Key client stakeholders
- Recommended follow-up timing
- Internal owner for membership conversion

FMS-0026 does not create memberships or billing workflows. It defines the closeout standard that prepares the conversation.

## Building Handover Meeting

Each completed prime contract should include a building handover meeting when practical.

Recommended meeting agenda:

- Project completion summary
- Installed fire protection systems
- Important as-built documents
- P.Eng. and contractor compliance records
- Product information and warranties
- Asset register review
- Inspection and maintenance expectations
- Service transition options
- Building Protection Passport™ overview
- FMS membership opportunity
- QR code or digital access explanation
- Questions and next steps

The meeting should produce a handover record or notes that become part of the internal building file.

## Digital Handover Process

Recommended process:

1. Confirm project completion.
2. Confirm the building exists in FMS or create a future migration task.
3. Collect required closeout deliverables.
4. Classify records by package type.
5. Mark records as internal-only or client-visible.
6. Identify Passport records.
7. Build or update the asset register.
8. Review P.Eng. and contractor compliance records.
9. Review as-built documentation.
10. Add warranty and product information.
11. Draft service transition summary.
12. Draft FMS membership transition summary.
13. Prepare handover meeting materials.
14. Conduct handover meeting.
15. Record lifecycle conversion opportunities.
16. Assign follow-up ownership.

## QR Code Workflow

Future QR code workflows should connect physical or digital handover materials to the building's Passport access experience.

Recommended workflow:

- Generate a building-specific QR code after the Passport record is ready.
- Link the QR code to a secure building Passport or client access page.
- Include the QR code in handover packages, binders, or approved signage where appropriate.
- Ensure QR access respects permissions and does not expose internal-only data.
- Support QR replacement or revocation if access rules change.
- Record when and where QR references were issued.

FMS-0026 does not implement QR code generation. It defines the desired future operating standard.

## Lifecycle Conversion

Digital closeout should trigger lifecycle conversion review.

Each completed prime contract should be evaluated for:

- ITM service contract opportunity
- FMS membership opportunity
- Passport onboarding opportunity
- Advisory review opportunity
- Capital planning opportunity
- Retrofit or modernization opportunity
- Portfolio expansion opportunity

The conversion review should assign an owner, status, next action, and follow-up timing.

## Internal Workflow

1. Project team confirms closeout readiness.
2. Closeout coordinator gathers source records.
3. Technical reviewer validates compliance and as-built package quality.
4. FMS administrator prepares or updates building and Passport metadata.
5. Service lead reviews ITM transition fit.
6. Sales or account owner reviews membership and lifecycle opportunities.
7. Handover package is prepared.
8. Building handover meeting is scheduled and completed.
9. Client-visible records are confirmed.
10. Internal-only notes are protected.
11. Relationship status is updated.
12. Follow-up ownership is assigned.
13. Closeout completion is measured in future dashboards.

## Roles and Responsibilities

- Project manager: Owns project closeout readiness and source document collection.
- Project coordinator: Organizes deliverables and tracks missing records.
- Engineering reviewer: Confirms P.Eng. package completeness where applicable.
- Field or technical lead: Confirms installed system and asset register accuracy.
- FMS administrator: Structures building, asset, document, and Passport metadata.
- Service lead: Defines service transition and ITM opportunity notes.
- Sales or account owner: Defines membership and lifecycle conversion next steps.
- Leadership: Reviews closeout completion, conversion performance, and strategic relationship outcomes.
- Client representative: Receives handover materials and confirms next steps.

## Quality Checklist

Before closeout is complete, confirm:

- Building profile is created or migration task is recorded.
- Project summary is complete.
- Required deliverables are present or marked not applicable.
- P.Eng. package is reviewed where applicable.
- Contractor compliance package is reviewed.
- As-built documentation is organized and versioned.
- Product information is attached or referenced.
- Asset register is complete enough for service transition.
- Warranty package includes terms and expiry dates where available.
- Inspection schedule guidance is included.
- Passport records are identified.
- Client-visible and internal-only records are separated.
- Handover meeting is completed or intentionally waived.
- QR code workflow is prepared or marked future/not applicable.
- ITM and FMS membership transition notes are captured.
- Lifecycle conversion opportunities are reviewed.
- Internal owner and next follow-up are assigned.
